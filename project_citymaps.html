Research and Design on Map Culture

Infographic Design

Silk Road Stories

Designer: Wang Yifan
May, 2016

Maps, as tools for transmitting spatial information, play a crucial role in human understanding of nature, application of objective laws, and societal transformation. With the proliferation of electronic maps, the utility of maps has been amplified, while their artistic value has been relegated.

Indeed, beyond being mere carriers of information, maps are significant cultural information carriers. The depiction and presentation of maps over the ages, both domestically and internationally, are closely associated with cultural symbols. This is particularly true of the unique and self-contained artistic expressions of maps from different regions and cultural backgrounds during the manual cartography era. These maps, rich in regional cultural hues and respected as art pieces, still hold high aesthetic value to this day.

The design work "Silk Road Story" focuses on the cultural symbolic attributes and expressive language of maps. By tracing the development of cartography and analyzing the stylistic characteristics of maps from different regions and times, it explores new forms of map representation. It integrates the attribute of time into flat maps, unifying time and space into a holistic view of human civilization's progress under this space-time from a more macro perspective.

Top Sights Maps

Designer: Wang Yifan
May, 2016

Rurik is a Swedish designer who pays great attention to the details of the design. He asked me to do the layout design for his city travel maps. These maps showcase the most famous and worthwhile attractions of a city and are generally placed in the lobby of high-end hotels for people to read. Rurik's high requirements for design can be reflected in every small icon, color, outline, shadow, every detail is carefully designed. I am honored to have participated in some of these projects, I have learned a lot!
