Shengqinyuan - Virtual Zoo

Research / UX Design

This is my master's graduation design work. Shengqinyuan is a responsive virtual zoo website featuring animals depicted in images from Chinese traditional cultural art. This virtual zoo presents Chinese culture and art in an engaging way!

Background

My fondness for pets has always led to a particular interest in the study of animal imagery. Throughout my three years of graduate study, I was deeply influenced by the research and design practice on Chinese traditional design philosophy and symbol system. This inspired me to construct an animal world in the context of Chinese traditional culture for my graduation design.

Through discussions with my supervisor, it became increasingly clear that in the context of my graduation project, the design should combine traditional media with a more youthful, aesthetically engaging, and interactive approach to enhance communication and appeal. I decided to create a responsive virtual zoo website to present my research on animal imagery in Chinese traditional culture and art.

üîç Research

I gathered over 1,000 images related to animal themes and accompanying text descriptions from traditional Chinese cultural art. From this collection, I selected more than 300 images as initial samples to design the virtual zoo.

The virtual zoo is designed with pavilions based on the classifications from Er Ya, an ancient Chinese encyclopedia from the Han Dynasty. Each pavilion houses animal images drawn from traditional Chinese artworks, organized according to these classifications.

In line with ancient Chinese mapping traditions, the map is oriented with the north at the bottom and south at the top. The four cardinal directions‚Äîeast, south, west, and north‚Äîcorrespond to the four mythical creatures: the Azure Dragon, Vermilion Bird, White Tiger, and Black Tortoise. The animal pavilions are arranged in these four directions, reflecting this ancient symbolic system.

üñ•Ô∏è Wireframe & Prototype

My website features a clear and well-organized information architecture, supported by a structured database model. It efficiently showcases content across various categories and integrates the search function. I've developed a information architecture and user flow to ensure an intuitive user experience.

As a responsive website, I need to design interfaces for different devices. Since the main display platform for my design is the desktop, I adopted the graceful degradation approach. I first designed the desktop wireframes and then scaled them down and adapted them for mobile devices.

At the grand entrance in the south, visitors can obtain tickets to begin this wondrous adventure. The formidable beast area in the west is surrounded by walls, ensuring safety. Additionally, the zoo features an emergency aid station, some picturesque piers, and a cozy inn, along with other functional buildings, to ensure a comprehensive and delightful experience during visit.

The Chinese layout design, which evolved from bamboo slips, features a vertical arrangement from right to left. To highlight the characteristics of traditional Chinese culture, I use a vertical arrangement for shorter text sections, while for longer passages that require reading, I adopt the more familiar horizontal layout.

On the pavilion page, I aim to simulate the effect of a zoo through size contrasts and irregular arrangements, while also showcasing aesthetic appeal through carefully designed layouts.

I organized the research process and conclusions into a research report and designed brochures, newspapers, and business cards.

‚úèÔ∏è My Takeaways

  1. I learned to collect data through various methods.
    In this project, I spent a significant amount of time gathering and organizing information. I needed to classify, sort, and edit these materials, including cropping and extracting images. Initially, I started with scans from albums and picture books, then I accessed museum and archive websites to gather more images. Additionally, some images were from my own photography. Through this process, I developed a deeper understanding of these animal images.
  2. I learned about responsive website design.
    A responsive website can be viewed on both computers and mobile devices, automatically adjusting to different screen sizes. Designing a responsive site requires deciding the initial size to start from. I chose to begin with a large screen to provide more space for content display, a method known as graceful degradation. Content that fits on one screen on a large display might require scrolling on a mobile device, so adjustments in layout are necessary to accommodate different devices.
