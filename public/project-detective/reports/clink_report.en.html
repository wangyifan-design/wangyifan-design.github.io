Overview

The “Connection” experiment is a neural interface designed to synchronise human consciousness with a bionic cognition unit.

Human brain activity—especially visual, affective, and imagistic regions—is streamed through a brain-computer rig into the bionic network in real time.

Preliminary Results

  • Visual signal synchronisation delay ≈ 0.04 s;
  • Emotional feedback variance ±12%;
  • Strong human emotions trigger short-lived “affective loop” phenomena inside the bionic unit.

Recommendations

Limit each session to ≤ 10 minutes and avoid repeated daily experiments without authorisation.
