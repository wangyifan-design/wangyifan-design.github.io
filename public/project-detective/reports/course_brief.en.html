Course: Design Theory and Practice of Artificial Beings

Instructor: Prof. John Quincy

Program

Visual Communication & Intelligent Design Research Center

Credits: 3

Overview

The class investigates the intersection of artificial intelligence and art-making, focusing on how “creative artificial beings” exist within contemporary visual design and its ethical boundaries.

Designing an artificial being is not about copying humanity, but about questioning the conditions that assemble what we call “human.”

Syllabus Outline

  • Theory block: history of synthetic beings, perception models, and philosophical debates around artistic imitation;
  • Workshop block: students collaborate with prototype intelligent systems to build a “visual work with personality.”

Assessment

Students submit a final report describing how their project defines the boundary between “creator” and “created.”

Goal

Understand the ethical, aesthetic, and emotional logic of designing artificial beings.
