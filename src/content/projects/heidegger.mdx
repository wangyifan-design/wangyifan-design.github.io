artists: "Jiang Xavier, <b>Wang Yifan</b>, Yang Hong, Uradee Amnueypol"
overview: |
  This digital interactive video artwork examines how AI reshapes the meaning of images. Drawing on Heidegger's notion of technology as a "framework," it explores the process of visual decay and reinterpretation. Inspired by the traditional Chinese aesthetic of "Heaps of Brocade and Ash" (锦灰堆), the piece constructs a layered visual composition that reflects the tension between machine logic and cultural memory.

  ## 🧪 This is an experimental art practice!
  In recent years, AI has had a profound impact on artistic creation. This work visualizes AI's disruption of images,
  not only in form but also in meaning. I contributed to the conceptual design and technical direction.
</div>

<div class="content-block">
  <img class="img-small project-image" src="/img/heidegger/1-0.jpg" alt="Collage of layered imagery used in the installation" />
  Heidegger saw technology as a framework that distances humans from nature. This work reimagines that idea using AI's
  reinterpretation of image memory and decay.
</div>

<div class="content-block">
  <img class="img-small project-image" src="/img/heidegger/1-0-1.jpg" alt="Reference collage inspired by Heaps of Brocade and Ash aesthetic" />
  **Heaps of Brocade and Ash** is a visual tradition of disorder and repair, reuniting fragments of the past to produce
  new beauty. It inspired how we layered AI-altered images to simulate deterioration and poetic recomposition.
</div>

<div class="content-block">
  <img class="project-image" src="/img/heidegger/1-6.jpg" alt="AI patina visual study combining layered textures" />
  **AI patina** is our term for AI's erosion and mutation of iconic images. We developed iterative AI techniques to
  generate glitch-like layering effects, producing visual entanglement and loss.
</div>

<div class="content-block">
  <img class="project-image" src="/img/heidegger/1-5.jpg" alt="Somatosensory installation view with participant interaction" />
  This piece includes a somatosensory interactive installation. Viewers alter the visuals with motion or gestures,
  showing how human presence still reshapes the technological framework.
